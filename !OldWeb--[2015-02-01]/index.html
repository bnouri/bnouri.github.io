About me: 

Behzad Nouri received the B. Eng. degree in Electronics Engineering from Tabriz University, Iran, and M.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering from Carleton University, Ottawa, Canada. He is currently working toward the Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering in the Department of Electronics at Carleton University.

His research interests include computer-aided design of VLSI circuits, modeling and simulation of high-speed interconnects MOR techniques for linear/nonlinear dynamical systems, and numerical algorithms.

Mr. Nouri was the recipient of the Best Paper Award presented at the 15th IEEE Workshop on Signal Propagation on Interconnects (2011) and Ontario Graduate Scholarship Award (2010), Dean of Graduate Studies Academic Excellence Scholarship (2008-2011), Graduate Departmental Excellence Scholarship (2008-2012) and other prestigious scholarships during his graduate studies.

A few years ago, I received a (gift) watch from a project manager in Alcatel (branch in Hanover, Germany). It was a kind token of appreciation for the role I had in one of our joint technical projects (on HDSL pair gain systems). I accepted the gift as it practically had no financial value. However, I valued and kept it in the past few years because of the wise message it bears as my friend explained. I shared its photo in the following figure (shown below to the left).

It symbolizes the message that, a successful industrial process starts from you ideas and thoughts (Head), then takes your tireless effort of engineering, prototyping and implementation (hand) to become what you can call it a product. Hence it can be mass-produced by undergoing an industrial process (triangles and other shapes). The self-satisfaction (heart) and economical turn-over will come after going through all these stages of challenging and effort.
