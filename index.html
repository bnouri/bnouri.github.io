My IEEE Biography

Behzad Nouri (S’08-M’14) received the B.Eng. degree in electronics engineering from the University of Tabriz, Tabriz, Iran, in 1988, and the M.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in electrical and computer engineering from Carleton University, Ottawa, Canada in 2008 and 2014, respectively.

He served in various capacities in the research labs and telecommunication industries, including the Iran Telecommunication Research Center, Tehran, Iran, from 1988 to 1990, Iran Communication Industries Inc., Tehran, Iran, from 1990 to 1999, and T-Telecom (Alcatel agent), Tehran, Iran, from 1999 to 2002. Since 2014, he has been a Post-Doctoral Research Fellow and a Research Adjunct Professor with the Department of Electronics Engineering at Carleton University. His current research interests include the computer-aided design of VLSI circuits, signal/power integrity analysis, nonlinear circuits, model-order reduction, data-driven macromodeling, parameter-varying systems, uncertainty quantification, and numerical algorithms for circuit simulation.

Dr. Nouri besides holding various prestigious scholarships during his graduate studies was the recipient of the University-Gold-Medal for his outstanding doctoral work in 2014. Also, he was the recipient of the Best Transactions Paper Award from the IEEE Component, Packaging and Manufacturing Technology in 2013 and the Best Paper Award from the IEEE Workshop on Signal and Power Integrity (SPI) in 2011 and 2018.

His contribution in perpetuating a positive learning environment, and in implementing innovative teaching practices in electronics department was recognized with the departmental teaching assistance excellence awards (2011, 2012) and a nomination (by the department of electronics) for the university outstanding teaching assistant award (2013).
